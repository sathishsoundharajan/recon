## D. Open Box Inspection

- a. An open box inspection will be conducted to establish a baseline for a reasonable tolerance of Concealed Damage that could be realized during the Delivery and Installation process. Concealed Damage is defined as any product damage that is not immediately visible or apparent upon a reasonable inspection by the carrier or their representatives at the time of transfer of ownership from Samsung to the carrier, at time of receipt at RXOLM's warehouse, at the time of transfer of ownership from RXOLM to their representative/Delivery Agent.
- b. On a quarterly basis (four (4) times annually), Samsung will conduct an Open Box Inspection at Samsung Warehouses. During this Open Box Inspection process; Samsung will identify, stage and conduct an Open Box inspection on a minimum of 10% of volume planned to be tendered to RXOLM over a 30-Day period.
- c. Inspection Procedure: RXOLM is encouraged to have representation onsite at Samsung's Warehouse during the inspection period, but is not required. If RXOLM elects to not have representation present for the inspection; they agree to, in good faith, accept the findings as shared by Samsung.
  - i. Opening and removing the product from factory packaging to inspect the contents
  - ii. Assessing the condition of the item for any visible damage
  - iii. Document any pre-existing defects or damages
  - iv. Take a minimum of three (3) high-quality photographs of the item from multiple angles, including box damage, product damage, and any observed damage as applicable.
  - v. If product is found to be in good condition; Samsung will repackage the product in original packaging and stage for transfer to RXOLM.
